# Phase 4 — Distribution Strategy Synthesis

Goal: compress everything from Phases 1-3 into a **replicable playbook** — the deliverable a PMM, founder, growth lead, or investor can act on without re-doing the research.

This phase is mostly judgment, not collection. You should write 80% less than you did in Phase 1, but every sentence should be load-bearing.

## What synthesis adds beyond the prior phases

- **Pattern recognition** — naming the strategy ("inverse-funnel SEO", "PLG-with-sales-overlay", "founder-led distribution")
- **Causal hypotheses** — connecting timeline events to outcomes
- **Ranked priorities** — what mattered most, what was theater
- **Transferable lessons** — what a different team could copy, what's locked to this team's unique advantages
- **A defensible point of view** — what you'd actually recommend doing about this

## Synthesis structure

### 1. The one-line strategy

In ≤25 words, name the strategy. Examples:
- "Founder-led Twitter funneling into a category-defining SEO comparison-page moat, monetized via sales-led enterprise once PLG fills top-of-funnel."
- "Bottom-up dev adoption via free OSS + GitHub stars, converted to paid via cloud-hosted version, expanded via team plans."
- "Marketplace cold-start solved by hand-recruited supply in one city, then 3-month exclusive demand acquisition before opening second city."

If you can't summarize in 25 words, you don't understand it yet.

### 2. The channel-mix matrix

```markdown
| Channel | % of effort | Paid/Organic | When activated | Why it works for them |
|---------|-------------|--------------|----------------|----------------------|
| SEO comparison pages | 35% | Organic | Month 0 | Pre-built before launch, captures bottom-funnel intent |
| Founder Twitter | 25% | Organic | Month -2 | Founder is a recognized voice in category |
| Product Hunt + PR | 5% | Earned | Launch week | One-time fuel; not repeatable |
| Google Ads (branded + comp) | 15% | Paid | Month 12 | Fund-raised; defends rankings |
| Affiliate program | 10% | Paid (revshare) | Month 18 | Compounds; leverages existing community |
| Integration listings | 5% | Organic | Month 14 | Free distribution from partner platforms |
| Sales-led enterprise | 5% | Outbound | Month 20 | Lands the 5-figure ACV deals top-of-funnel can't |
```

Effort % is judgment, not data — anchor it in observable signals (post counts, ad spend bands, hire counts, page counts).

### 3. Messaging evolution

Use Wayback snapshots from Phase 2 to track headline + sub-headline + primary CTA at 3-5 inflection points. Then characterize the evolution.

```markdown
| Date | Headline | Positioning | Primary CTA |
|------|----------|-------------|-------------|
| 2022-09 | "Stop manually tagging invoices" | Pain-led, narrow | "Try free" |
| 2023-04 | "AI-powered AP automation" | Category-led | "Get demo" |
| 2024-02 | "The finance OS for modern teams" | Platform-led | "Start free trial" |
| 2025-08 | "AP, AR, and procurement on one platform" | Suite-led | "Talk to sales" |
```

**Pattern**: pain → category → platform → suite. Classic expansion narrative as ACV grew.

### 4. The compounding loops

Identify what's working *without ongoing input*. These are the assets a competitor cannot easily catch up to:
- Backlink moat from 47 SEO articles (compounds with time)
- 1.2K G2 reviews (social proof flywheel)
- 8K-strong Slack community (peer-led support reduces CAC and churn)
- Open-source repo with 12K stars (free distribution + recruiting)
- 200+ integration listings (each is passive distribution)

For each, note: when it started, how long it took to compound, what the company is doing to feed it.

### 5. The non-obvious lever

Every good teardown has one finding that surprises the reader. This is often:
- A "hidden in plain sight" channel doing more work than people realize
- A pricing or packaging move that unlocked everything
- A partnership that pre-loaded their go-to-market
- A founder behavior (podcast circuit, writing) that's underrated
- A product wedge that was bait for a different sale

Force yourself to identify exactly one. If you find more, pick the most impactful.

### 6. The "Copy This in 90 Days" plan

Take everything above and produce a ranked, actionable list. Each item: move → effort → expected impact → how to know if it's working.

```markdown
| # | Move | Effort (1-5) | Impact (1-5) | Leading indicator |
|---|------|--------------|--------------|-------------------|
| 1 | Build 12 SEO comparison pages targeting top competitor brand terms | 3 | 5 | Pages indexed within 30 days; 100+ organic clicks/wk by day 60 |
| 2 | Stand up founder Twitter with 4 posts/wk, technical depth | 2 | 4 | 500 followers in 60 days, 3 inbound demo requests |
| 3 | Coordinate Product Hunt launch with pre-warmed list of 200 emails | 4 | 3 | Top 5 of day; 800 signups in 48h |
| 4 | Public changelog with bi-weekly cadence | 1 | 3 | 10% of new signups citing "active development" in survey |
| 5 | Launch affiliate program with 30% lifetime, simple terms | 3 | 4 | 20 affiliates onboarded by day 90, 5% of revenue attributed by month 6 |
| ... | | | | |
```

Be opinionated. Don't list 20 things. 5-10 ranked moves is the sweet spot.

### 7. What you wouldn't copy

Equally important. Often:
- Things that worked because of unique founder advantage (audience, network, prior exit)
- Things that required capital you don't have (Series A → paid acquisition phase)
- Things that worked in a now-saturated market (PH launch in 2022 ≠ today)
- Things that look impressive but didn't actually drive growth (PR coverage, awards)

State which moves *not* to copy, and why.

### 8. Open questions / gaps

Be explicit about what you couldn't verify. This calibrates the reader's confidence and creates a research agenda for round 2:
- What's their actual CAC by channel? (would need to talk to ex-employees)
- Is the affiliate program actually working or theater? (would need conversion data)
- Why did pricing drop in 2023? (activation issue or competitive response?)
- How does the enterprise motion actually work? (sales process is opaque)

## Quality bar

A strong synthesis:
- Could be skimmed in 5 minutes and acted on
- Has at least one finding that contradicts the company's own marketing narrative
- Connects timeline → funnel → strategy with explicit causal hypotheses
- Names the strategy (not just describes it)
- Forces a ranked recommendation, not a menu

A weak synthesis:
- Lists everything found in earlier phases without compression
- Uses generic categories ("they do content marketing")
- Hedges every claim ("they may or may not be doing X")
- Has no point of view on what to do
- Doesn't distinguish what worked from what's just present

## Final check before delivery

- [ ] One-line strategy statement is in the report
- [ ] Channel-mix matrix totals ~100% of effort
- [ ] Messaging evolution shows actual copy diffs from Wayback
- [ ] At least one "non-obvious lever" is called out
- [ ] 90-day plan has 5-10 ranked moves with leading indicators
- [ ] "What I wouldn't copy" section exists and is honest
- [ ] Open questions list is specific (not just "we'd want more data")
- [ ] Every major claim has a citation back to a Phase 1 footprint
